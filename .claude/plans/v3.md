# Plan ÔÇö Averrow v3: Engine-Based Customer Tenant Platform

> Status: **Research draft**. Not for execution. Captures the v3 vision, current-state findings, gaps, and open research tracks. Operator wants more research before any building starts.

---

## Context

Averrow v2 ships a powerful threat-intelligence platform for the Averrow team to operate. It works ÔÇö 40 feeds (33 enabled per 2026-05-06 audit), a self-supervising agent mesh, 9,675 brands, 234,691 threats, threat-actor correlation, auto-takedowns, AI cost discipline at ~$150/mo. But its information architecture is module-shaped: a customer logs in and sees a federation of independent dashboards (threats, alerts, brands, observatory, takedowns) without a coherent narrative. A T&S analyst opening the platform Monday morning has no answer to "what should I focus on today."

The v3 thesis: **the platform's job is to find threats from raw data, then use AI to correlate them into bigger patterns ÔÇö threat actors, MO, pivots, AI-generated content fingerprints, and the brands those attacks target.** v2 has the parts; v3 is the architecture that ties them together for a paying customer's threat-intel or trust-and-safety team.

The operator has also flagged a market drift: Averrow is positioned as if it's chasing Netflix and Google, when its actual sweet spot is mid-market brands with limited T&S capability and weak email security posture. v3 must reflect that focus from day one.

This plan captures the v3 vision, maps current capabilities to it, identifies gaps, and frames the research tracks needed before any code is written.

---

## 1. The v3 vision (operator's words, restated)

v3 is laid out around **six engines**, each with one job:

| Engine | Job | Why it's its own engine |
|---|---|---|
| **Ingest** | Acquire raw data | Pure data plane ÔÇö should never know about brands, customers, or correlation |
| **Correlation** | Find patterns: threat actors, MO, pivots, AI-content fingerprints, brand-target binding | The intelligence layer; AI's home |
| **Presentation** | Show threat actors, brand impact/health, intel ÔÇö for *customer* teams | Outcome-driven, not module-driven |
| **Mitigation** | Takedowns + auto-resubmit on pivot + provider scoring | Closes the loop |
| **Other** | Spam trap / honeypot / abuse mailbox (Averrow master + per-tenant) | Asymmetric signal capture; outside the standard ingest path |
| **Integrations** | SIEM, TIP, email security, SSO, RBAC, customer resources | Plugs into the customer's existing stack |

The differentiator is the **correlation engine** ÔÇö AI-powered actor pattern detection tied to brand context. Most platforms ingest, classify per-row, and present. Averrow's claim is that the same actor pivoting from provider A to B becomes one story, not two unrelated alerts.

The architectural fork: v3 is either a **clean replacement** for v2, or v3 is the **customer tenant app** while v2 stays as the **Averrow staff back-office**. Operator's rough thinking leans toward the second. This plan treats it as an open structural decision (┬º5).

## 4. v3 architecture ÔÇö proposed shape

### 4.1 The six engines as code packages

```
packages/
  trust-radar-v3/                # New worker. v2 stays as-is.
    src/
      ingest/                    # Ôåö feeds/, threat-feeds.ts, dmarc-receiver.ts
      correlation/               # Ôåö agents/ (nexus, attributor, sentinel, etc.)
      presentation/              # API handlers ÔÇö read-only
      mitigation/                # Ôåö sparrow, evidence-assembler, takedowns
      other/                     # Ôåö spam-trap, honeypot, abuse-mailbox/
      integrations/              # SSO, SIEM, TIP, email-security, webhooks
      platform/                  # Shared: agent mesh, FC, cost guard, cubes, KV cache
  averrow-tenant/                # New React app ÔÇö customer-facing
    src/                         # Outcome-driven IA: threat-actors, brand-health, intel
  averrow-ops/                   # v2's averrow-ui rebadged for staff back-office
    src/                         # Admin dashboards, FC, agent monitoring, Pathfinder
```

**Why this shape:**
- Engines are *namespaces*, not separate workers. One worker, one DB, clean folder boundaries.
- The customer tenant app and the staff ops app share the data layer. Different IA, different RBAC scope, different design language allowed.

## 7. Research track A ÔÇö Abuse mailbox

**Status: operator has shared the design (co-developed previously at Bolster AI). Captured below.**

### 7.1 The design ÔÇö customer-branded abuse mailbox (single product)

Per Bolster precedent, exemplified by DocuSign's `verify@docusign.com` listed on `docusign.com/safety#report-fraud`:

- Customer publishes a customer-branded abuse / verify / report-fraud address on their own trust & safety page (e.g., `verify@docusign.com`, `report-phishing@brand.com`)
- Customer's existing email infrastructure (Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, etc.) auto-forwards messages received at that address to a per-tenant Averrow ingestion endpoint
- Averrow processes, classifies, and responds ÔÇö outbound from the customer's domain
- Reporter only ever sees the customer's brand throughout
- Averrow stays invisible to reporters

**No public Averrow mailbox.** Averrow does not market a `report-to-averrow` address. The product is sold to brands; the brands market the address to their reporters via their own T&S pages.

**The two-response pattern:**

- **First response (instant):** templated, customer-branded acknowledgment. States the 24-hour expectation for the second response.
- **Background processing:** parse ÔÇö extract URLs, domains, brand mentions, IPs, embedded logos. Queue for classification.
- **Second response (Ôëñ24 hours):** templated message with best-practice guidance (mark as spam, contact IT) without disclosing findings. Includes Bolster-style soft disclaimer.

**Inbound + outbound infrastructure:**

- **Inbound: zero DNS work for customer.** Customer's existing email infra forwards the abuse address to a per-tenant Averrow ingestion endpoint (alias-forward rule). No MX changes required.
- **Outbound: dual-path via managed transactional email provider** (Bolster used SendGrid):
  - **Primary ÔÇö domain authentication:** customer adds CNAMEs to their DNS pointing at the email provider's signing keys (standard "domain authentication" flow). Outbound is `@customer.com` or `@safety.customer.com`, properly DKIM/SPF-aligned. Reporter sees real customer email.
  - **Fallback ÔÇö averrow-hosted with customer branding:** customer can't or won't add DNS records. Averrow sends from `abusereply_<tenant>@averrow.com` with customer-customizable copy, logo, and templates. Required footer: "Powered by Averrow" + link. Lowest-friction path; highlighted as the default during onboarding.

**Pricing:** standalone SKU, volume-tiered (Ôëñ5K / Ôëñ10K / Ôëñ25K / 25K+ reports per month), auto-upgrade with overage charge.

### 7.2 Why this design wins

1. **Customer acquires their own reporters via their own brand.** Averrow doesn't drive traffic to a public mailbox ÔÇö customers do that on their own T&S pages, leveraging brand reputation they already have. **Eliminates the "Tier 1 reporter acquisition" problem entirely.**
2. **Two value flows from one ingestion stream:**
   - **Service** ÔÇö customer offers a credible report-fraud channel; reporters get acknowledgment + best-practice guidance
   - **Cross-tenant intel** ÔÇö every customer's reports feed the master correlation pool. Crowd-sourced honeypot at the scale of all customers' reporter bases combined.
3. **Aggregate cross-tenant intel is the moat.** As more brands adopt, intel compounds. Threat actors reusing infrastructure across brands get caught faster. Makes ┬º5.5 (cross-tenant intel sharing model) **structurally part of the product**, not a separate add-on.
4. **White-label invisibility preserves customer brand trust** with reporters. Outbound looks native; reporter never knows Averrow exists.
5. **Asymmetric response keeps detection IP private** ÔÇö avoids creating a side-channel for adversaries to test their kits' detection status.
6. **Inbound is zero-DNS for customers.** Just an alias-forward rule in their existing email server. Outbound DNS work is optional via the fallback path. Lowest-friction enterprise-grade onboarding in the category.
7. **Can ship standalone before v3 ships** ÔÇö early revenue path. No Tier 1 / Tier 2 split means simpler scoping than previously estimated.

### 7.4 Decisions captured (cumulative)

- **Inbound model: customer-domain alias-forward** (Bolster precedent). Customer publishes a customer-branded address on their T&S page (e.g., `verify@docusign.com`); their existing email infrastructure forwards to a per-tenant Averrow ingestion endpoint. **No DNS changes required for inbound.**
- **Inbound formats: dual.** Reporters submit either:
  - **Forward-as-attachment (preferred)** ÔÇö original email attached as RFC822; headers + body + attachments preserved. Highest-fidelity intel.
  - **URL-only paste** ÔÇö reporter copies a suspicious URL into a plain email. Lower fidelity but still actionable. Parser must handle both. Customer T&S page copy should explicitly request the attachment format (DocuSign's wording: "forward the suspicious email as an attachment or copy the URL").
- **Customer-facing T&S page copy: Averrow-provided templates.** Onboarding ships with paste-ready snippets the customer drops onto their T&S page. Wording follows the DocuSign pattern: customer-branded ("Our teams will analyze it..."), Averrow stays invisible. Customer can edit but template gets them shipped same-day.
- **Outbound model: dual-path via managed transactional provider.**
  - **Primary** ÔÇö customer adds CNAMEs (domain authentication); outbound is `@customer.com` via the provider. Reporter sees real customer email.
  - **Fallback** ÔÇö averrow-hosted (`abusereply_<tenant>@averrow.com`) with customer-customizable copy + logo + "Powered by Averrow" footer. Lowest friction; default during onboarding.
- **Outbound provider: Resend** (selected ┬º7.4.1.4 after ┬º2.5 audit confirmed it's already wired in production via `briefing-email.ts` + `invite-email.ts`). 4-week deliverability A/B against Postmark + Mailgun runs at start of v0; Mailgun is the EU-residency fallback.
- **Spoofed-report + DDoS defense at v1.** Not to *reject* ÔÇö to *route*. Attacker submissions are intel, including headers. Defense is classify + queue under load, not block. Junk mail from attackers is feature, not bug.
- **Volume queueing.** Spike-tolerant FIFO queue with backpressure built in.
- **Latency expectation: 24 hours.** Stated in first auto-response.
- **Pricing: standalone SKU, volume-tiered.**
  - Tier A: Ôëñ5,000 reports/month
  - Tier B: Ôëñ10,000 reports/month
  - Tier C: Ôëñ25,000 reports/month
  - Tier D: 25,000+ (custom / enterprise)
  - Standalone SKU. Billable independent of v3 subscription.
  - **Tier overage handling: auto-upgrade with overage charge.** No hard caps.
- **Tenant inbox UX (list view):** sortable + searchable. Columns: timestamp, sender, subject, shortened body, TTPs, **determination** (deliberately *not* "verdict").
- **Tenant inbox UX (drill-down):** all captured details + threats + ability to view the full original email.
- **Auto-takedown default-on, per-tenant + per-confidence-threshold.** Per-tenant on/off toggle plus per-confidence-threshold tuning. Per-detection-type granularity deferred to v2.
- **Spoofed-from-domain in customer-facing intel.** Attackers spoof legitimate domains in From: headers. We do **not** publish the spoofed domain as fact. Surface as originating-IP / actor / pattern.
- **Disclaimer language (Bolster posture):** soft, plain-language ÔÇö "this is a determination based on information and data gathered" + "always be vigilant and use best practices with all email." Legal review of actual copy before launch.
- **Raw email retention (locked):**
  - **Hot (R2): 30 days** raw email + attachments. Quarantined; tenant-authenticated retrieval only. Malware-scanned on ingest.
  - **Warm: 90 days** parsed indicators + extracted IOCs + classifier outputs. No raw email content.
  - **Cold / indefinite:** confirmed threat hashes, IOC fingerprints, actor links. No PII.
  - **Regulated-industry override** (financial services, healthcare) negotiable per contract.
  - **Auto-purge on tenant offboarding:** 30 days post-contract; intel-side derived hashes/IOCs anonymized + retained.
- **Cross-tenant intel sharing model (locked):** opt-in per tenant, with two independent toggles:
  - `contribute_to_aggregate` ÔÇö tenant's anonymized abuse intel feeds the cross-tenant correlation pool. Default: **off**.
  - `consume_aggregate_intel` ÔÇö tenant sees anonymized cross-tenant intel patterns in their dashboard. Default: **off**.
  - Both toggles default off but **onboarding includes a value-prop step nudging opt-in** with clear explanation of what flows where.
  - Independent toggles allow asymmetric participation (contribute-only altruist; consume-only enterprise / regulated tenant). Both states are valid product configurations.
  - Both toggle states + change history captured in DPA addendum + audit log. Anonymization floor + k-anonymity threshold = build-phase decision.

### 7.4.1 Remaining open questions ÔÇö resolved

Four real open questions (Q4 cross-tenant sharing already locked in ┬º7.4; not repeated here).

#### 7.4.1.1 Disclaimer language scope ÔÇö hybrid

**Recommendation: hybrid.** Legal-load-bearing language stays Averrow-wide; customer voice lives in a preamble.

| Element | Owner | Editable? |
|---|---|---|
| Customer preamble ("From the [Brand] T&S team...") | Customer | Yes ÔÇö onboarding template, customer-editable |
| Determination block ("This is a determination based on information gathered...") | Averrow legal | **No** ÔÇö locked, single legal review |
| Best-practice block ("Always be vigilant...") | Averrow legal | **No** ÔÇö locked |
| Footer (primary domain-auth path) | Customer | Sender domain implies brand; no Averrow surface |
| Footer (fallback path) | Averrow | "Powered by Averrow" + link to ┬º7.4.1.2 page |

**Rationale:** legal review is tighter when the load-bearing copy is uniform across tenants. The customer's voice is preserved through the preamble (the part reporters notice). One legal review covers all tenants for the locked blocks.

**Regulated-industry override:** finserv / healthcare customers may negotiate per-tenant disclaimer block overrides via contract amendment with per-change Averrow legal review. Same pattern as the ┬º7.4 retention override.

**Build action:** JSON schema for tenant-side preamble + two immutable Averrow blocks. i18n deferred to v2 (English-only at launch).

#### 7.4.1.2 "Powered by Averrow" footer link ÔÇö dedicated transparency page

**Only applies to fallback outbound** (`abusereply_<tenant>@averrow.com`). Primary domain-auth path never surfaces Averrow.

**Recommendation: dedicated page** at `averrow.com/notify` (or `/why-this-email`). Not the homepage ÔÇö a B2B marketing page is the wrong reading for a confused reporter.

**Page contents (~150 words):**
- Why you got this email ÔÇö you reported a suspicious message to [Brand]
- What Averrow is, in 2 sentences
- What we did with your report ÔÇö classified, may have initiated takedown (deliberately vague ÔÇö preserves ┬º7.1 asymmetric-response IP)
- [Brand]'s involvement ÔÇö they engaged us to analyze reports
- Privacy contact + right-to-deletion link (points to ┬º7.4.1.3 endpoints)

**Side benefit:** this page doubles as the **GDPR Art. 13 transparency notice** required by ┬º7.4.1.3. One page = reporter education + legal compliance.

**Implementation:** static page, edge-cached. URL baked into fallback footer at tenant-provisioning time.

#### 7.4.1.3 Submitter PII handling ÔÇö operational + contractual model

Five sub-decisions:

1. **Lawful basis (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f)):** legitimate interest ÔÇö cybercrime prevention. Balancing test documented in DPA. Customer's T&S page copy ships with a notice link to ┬º7.4.1.2 page.

2. **Data minimization:** collect only what's in the inbound report (headers, body, attachments). No enrichment of submitter identity beyond source IP for rate-limiting. After 30-day raw-retention (┬º7.4 R2 quarantine), submitter email is retained only as `submitter_email_hash` (SHA-256, salted per-tenant).

3. **Cross-border / data residency:** customer chooses processing region at onboarding. R2 bucket region pinned per tenant. **Open build-phase concern:** D1 has no per-row region pinning ÔÇö EU-residency customers may require a separate `trust-radar-eu` D1 instance with anonymized-only cross-region intel sync. Flag for Phase 5 sequencing.

4. **DPA addendum:** standard processor agreement template, ships pre-signed for self-serve onboarding. Custom DPAs for enterprise. Sub-processor list: Cloudflare (host), Resend (outbound ÔÇö see ┬º7.4.1.4; Mailgun added if EU residency tenant), VirusTotal (malware scan), Anthropic (Haiku for classifier). Customer sees + accepts at signup.

5. **Right to access / deletion endpoints (anonymize-and-retain model):**
   - `GET /api/abuse-reports/by-submitter/{hashed_email}` ÔÇö auth via one-time email-confirmation link to the claimed address
   - `DELETE /api/abuse-reports/by-submitter/{hashed_email}` ÔÇö triggers **anonymize, not purge**: drops submitter row, nulls `submitter_email_hash`, retains extracted IOCs / classifier output / threat-actor links
   - Preserves intel value (hashed URLs, infrastructure, AI fingerprints) without retaining submitter identity. Consistent with ┬º7.4 cold/indefinite IOC retention.

**Why anonymize-and-retain rather than full purge:** the cross-tenant intel pool (┬º7.4 contribute toggle) is the moat. Full purge of intel on submitter request would let attackers weaponize GDPR DSARs to scrub their own reports. Anonymization satisfies GDPR (no identifiable data retained) while preserving the threat record.

#### 7.4.1.4 Outbound provider ÔÇö start with already-wired Resend; A/B-confirm before launch

**Reality check (per ┬º2.5 audit):** Resend is **already in production** for `briefing-email.ts` and `invite-email.ts`. `RESEND_API_KEY` is configured. Switching providers means rewiring two existing modules + adding a new sub-processor.

| Provider | Domain auth | EU residency | Cost @ 50K/mo | Deliverability | Wired today? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Resend** | Ô£ô CNAME flow | EU regions added 2025 | ~$85/mo | Strong; improved 2024-25 | Ô£ô in production |
| Postmark | Ô£ô mature | EU servers available | ~$115/mo | Industry-leading | No |
| SendGrid | Ô£ô standard | EU sub-processor | ~$90/mo | Mid (volume-dependent) | No |
| Mailgun | Ô£ô standard | Ô£ô explicit EU region | ~$95/mo | Mid-high | No |
| SES | Ô£ô DNS-based | Ô£ô region-pickable | ~$5/mo | Worst defaults | No |

**Revised recommendation: keep Resend at v1, A/B-confirm before public launch.**

Reasoning shift from prior draft:
- The earlier Postmark recommendation was made before ┬º2.5 audit confirmed Resend is the platform's existing transactional provider
- Adding Postmark introduces dual-provider operational overhead (two API keys, two sub-processor agreements, two reliability SLAs)
- Resend's deliverability has matured significantly through 2024-25; the 2023 "young, improving" framing is stale
- Resend's CNAME-based domain authentication is what the ┬º7.1 primary-path needs

**v1 plan: Resend, with explicit deliverability gate.**
- Build the abuse-mailbox outbound on Resend
- Run a 4-week pre-launch deliverability A/B against Postmark + Mailgun on simulated abuse-reply traffic
- Inbox-placement gate: if Resend lands ÔëÑ95% in inbox (not spam) for the customer-domain CNAME flow, ship on Resend
- If <95%, switch to Postmark for abuse-replies only (briefing emails stay on Resend ÔÇö different deliverability requirements)

**EU sovereignty fallback** (unchanged): Mailgun for EU-residency-sensitive tenants if Resend EU coverage is insufficient at customer's contract date.

**Sub-processor implication:** Resend stays on the DPA sub-processor list (already in production); Postmark added only if the inbox-placement gate fails.

### 7.5 What can ship standalone (decoupled from v3)

Single product, single phase. No Tier 1 / Tier 2 split:

- Per-tenant Cloudflare Email Routing ingestion endpoints
- Inbound MIME parser supporting **both forward-as-attachment (RFC822 message extraction) and URL-only paste** + DKIM-Auth-Results capture + submitter reputation table + rate limiting at v1
- Volume queue with backpressure at v1
- `abuse_reports` table + R2 raw storage with 30-day quarantine + malware scanning
- Rule-based classifier first pass; Haiku for ambiguous cases
- IOC extraction + integration with existing `threats` table and `lookalike_domains`
- Logo similarity reuse from `lookalike-scanner.ts`
- Two-response flow with 24-hour expectation, customer-branded templates
- Outbound via SendGrid-class provider ÔÇö both paths (domain-auth primary + averrow-hosted fallback)
- Disclaimer language (legal-reviewed before launch)
- Per-tenant auto-takedown toggle + confidence-threshold setting
- Minimal standalone tenant inbox UI (list + drill-down) for pre-v3 functionality

**Engineering effort: ~6-8 weeks standalone** ÔÇö collapsing the prior Tier 1 / Tier 2 split eliminates duplicated work and saves ~3-4 weeks vs the previous estimate.

**UX dependency:** the inbox UI (timestamp / sender / subject / body / TTPs / determination + drill-down) needs a home. Recommend ~2 weeks of minimal standalone tenant inbox view to make the product fully shippable pre-v3. Inbox UI ports into v3 customer tenant app when that lands.

### 7.6 Concerns flagged (updated)

1. **Legal duty-to-warn ÔÇö Bolster posture works.** Soft disclaimer + "be vigilant" framing. **Still requires legal review of actual template language before launch.**
2. **Outbound friction ÔÇö resolved by dual-path design.** Domain-auth (zero indicator design needed) + averrow-hosted fallback (customer fully customizes branding).
3. **Adversarial submissions ÔÇö v1 scope confirmed.** Spoofed defense + DDoS protection + queueing all land at v1.
4. **PII / GDPR handling ÔÇö design resolved (┬º7.4.1.3); legal review pending.** Retention windows locked (┬º7.4), DPA addendum model + anonymize-and-retain deletion endpoints + cross-border / data-residency model designed (┬º7.4.1.3). Remaining: legal review of DPA template + Postmark/Mailgun sub-processor agreement + D1 EU-residency engineering (separate `trust-radar-eu` instance ÔÇö flagged Phase 5).
5. **Cost discipline at Tier D (25K+ reports/month).** Per-tenant cost monitoring + overage tracking. Existing budget guard handles platform-wide; need per-tenant slice.
6. **Attacker payloads in storage.** R2 quarantine + malware scanner integration. v1 requirement.
7. **Cross-tenant intel is now the core mechanic, not a side-effect.** Means ┬º5.5 needs to be answered before this ships ÔÇö paid-tier vs default vs anonymized-only directly affects how the abuse mailbox product is positioned and contracted.
8. **Customer T&S page listing requires customer effort.** Product success depends on the customer publishing the abuse address on their own T&S page. If the customer doesn't do this (or buries it), the mailbox sees no traffic. Onboarding ships with paste-ready T&S page copy (DocuSign-pattern wording, customer-brandable) so customers can publish same-day. Concern shifts from "customer needs to write T&S copy" to "customer needs to deploy T&S copy" ÔÇö much smaller lift.
9. **Reporters who forward inline (not as attachment) lose headers.** DocuSign-style instructions ask for attachment-format, but real-world reporters often forward inline. Parser must degrade gracefully ÔÇö extract URLs / brands / IPs from the body even when full headers aren't available. Lower-fidelity classification, but still actionable intel.
10. **In-product abuse reporting and account appeals are separate workflows** (per DocuSign's T&S page split). v1 abuse mailbox handles email-reporting only. In-product abuse reporting (a "Report this" button inside the customer's own product) and account appeals workflows are out of scope for v1 but worth noting as adjacent products customers may eventually want.

### 8.1 What we know
- Pathfinder excludes 5000+ employees and a hardcoded list of service-provider domains (cloud / CDN / registrar / sec-vendor / mega-tech)

- **No firmographic data** on `brands` (no `employee_count_est`, `revenue_range`, `market_cap_usd`, `funding_stage`)

### 8.2 The key insight ÔÇö most "size" signal SHOULD already be in the platform (with caveats from ┬º8.6.1 audit)

A rough company-size proxy can be derived from existing data without any paid provider:
- **Tranco rank** ÔÇö web prominence (lower rank = more reach). **Audit caveat: 0% coverage today** ÔÇö `handleImportTranco` bug (┬º8.6.1). After ~1hr fix, this becomes a real Layer-1 signal.
- **Email security grade** ÔÇö A+/A correlates with mature security (= bigger team, bigger budget). **100% coverage.** ÔåÉ strongest live signal today
- **DNS / MX complexity** ÔÇö own MX vs Google Workspace / Microsoft 365 = signal. To be derived per ┬º8.7.1.
- **Threat count** ÔÇö bigger brands attract more impersonation. **19% coverage** (only 1,798 of 9,675 brands have non-zero threat_count).
- **Sector** ÔÇö already classified (15 categories). **31% coverage** today; backfilling at ~270 brands/day via `brand_enricher` (~25 days to drain backlog).
- **hq_country** ÔÇö already enriched. **28% coverage** today.
- **Registrar maturity** ÔÇö enterprise registrars (Markmonitor, CSC) vs SMB registrars (GoDaddy, Namecheap) = signal.

**Reality check:** of the 7 signals listed, only **email_security_grade is at 100% coverage today**. Tranco_rank is at 0% (broken). Sector + hq_country are below 35%. The ┬º8.2 thesis is directionally right ÔÇö these signals exist as columns ÔÇö but actual data coverage is a Phase 0 prerequisite, not a given.

```sql
-- Q1: Distribution of all brands by email security grade ├ù tranco rank quartile
-- Find which combination over-indexes for "weak security + meaningful reach"
SELECT
  email_security_grade,
  CASE
    WHEN tranco_rank IS NULL THEN 'no_rank'
    WHEN tranco_rank <= 1000 THEN 'top_1k'
    WHEN tranco_rank <= 10000 THEN 'top_10k'
    WHEN tranco_rank <= 100000 THEN 'top_100k'
    ELSE 'beyond_100k'
  END AS rank_tier,
  COUNT(*) AS brand_count,
  AVG(threat_count) AS avg_threat_count
FROM brands
WHERE monitoring_tier IS NOT NULL
GROUP BY email_security_grade, rank_tier
ORDER BY rank_tier, email_security_grade;

-- Q2: Existing sales_leads distribution by sector ├ù email grade ├ù score
-- Identify the empirical sweet-spot cluster
SELECT
  b.sector,
  b.email_security_grade,
  COUNT(*) AS lead_count,
  AVG(sl.score) AS avg_score,
  SUM(CASE WHEN sl.status = 'qualified' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS qualified_count,
  ROUND(100.0 * SUM(CASE WHEN sl.status = 'qualified' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) / COUNT(*), 1) AS qualified_rate_pct
FROM sales_leads sl
JOIN brands b ON sl.brand_id = b.id
WHERE sl.score >= 35
GROUP BY b.sector, b.email_security_grade
ORDER BY qualified_rate_pct DESC, lead_count DESC
LIMIT 50;

-- Q3: Tranco rank distribution of high-score leads
-- Where does the empirical sweet-spot sit in web-prominence terms?
SELECT
  CASE
    WHEN b.tranco_rank IS NULL THEN 'no_rank'
    WHEN b.tranco_rank <= 1000 THEN 'top_1k'
    WHEN b.tranco_rank <= 5000 THEN '1k_to_5k'
    WHEN b.tranco_rank <= 10000 THEN '5k_to_10k'
    WHEN b.tranco_rank <= 50000 THEN '10k_to_50k'
    WHEN b.tranco_rank <= 100000 THEN '50k_to_100k'
    ELSE 'beyond_100k'
  END AS rank_band,
  COUNT(*) AS lead_count,
  AVG(sl.score) AS avg_score,
  SUM(CASE WHEN sl.status = 'qualified' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS qualified
FROM sales_leads sl
JOIN brands b ON sl.brand_id = b.id
WHERE sl.score >= 50
GROUP BY rank_band
ORDER BY lead_count DESC;

-- Q4: Sectors over-indexing on weak email security (the customer-fit signal)
SELECT
  sector,
  COUNT(*) AS total_brands,
  SUM(CASE WHEN email_security_grade IN ('D', 'F') THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) AS weak_grade_brands,
  ROUND(100.0 * SUM(CASE WHEN email_security_grade IN ('D', 'F') THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) / COUNT(*), 1) AS weak_pct,
  AVG(threat_count) AS avg_threat_count
FROM brands
WHERE email_security_grade IS NOT NULL
  AND sector IS NOT NULL
GROUP BY sector
HAVING COUNT(*) >= 50
ORDER BY weak_pct DESC;

-- Q5: Geographic concentration of high-priority leads
SELECT
  b.hq_country,
  b.sector,
  COUNT(*) AS lead_count,
  AVG(sl.score) AS avg_score
FROM sales_leads sl
JOIN brands b ON sl.brand_id = b.id
WHERE sl.score >= 50
  AND b.hq_country IS NOT NULL
GROUP BY b.hq_country, b.sector
HAVING COUNT(*) >= 3
ORDER BY lead_count DESC, avg_score DESC
LIMIT 50;

-- Q6: How leads have trended over time ÔÇö is the funnel healthy?
SELECT
  DATE(sl.created_at) AS day,

FROM sales_leads sl
WHERE sl.created_at > datetime('now', '-90 days')
GROUP BY day
ORDER BY day DESC;
```

### 8.7 Layer 2 implementation specifics ÔÇö free open data sources

Concrete API patterns, sample queries, coverage estimates. Sequenced by leverage (highest first).

#### 8.7.1 DNS / MX firmographic signals (extension of Cartographer) ÔÇö HIGHEST LEVERAGE

**Already partly in our pipeline.** Zero new dependencies; extends existing code.

**The signal:** MX provider correlates with company size + security maturity.

| MX pattern | Implies | Size signal |
|---|---|---|
| `*.google.com`, `aspmx.l.google.com` | Google Workspace | SMB / lower-mid-market |
| `*.outlook.com`, `*.protection.outlook.com` | Microsoft 365 | Mid-market+ |
| `*.pphosted.com`, `*.ppe-hosted.com` | Proofpoint | Enterprise |
| `*.mimecast.com` | Mimecast | Enterprise |
| `*.barracudanetworks.com` | Barracuda | Mid-market |
| Own MX (matches brand domain) | Self-hosted | Either tech-savvy SMB or large enterprise (need cross-signal) |
| `*.mailgun.org`, `*.sendgrid.net` | Transactional only (probably not primary) | n/a |

**Already-classified providers:** `email-security.ts` already detects Proofpoint / Mimecast / Barracuda / KnowBe4 from MX. Extending to Google / Microsoft is straightforward.

**Cross-signal: SPF lookup count.** SPF records with >5 `include:` directives suggest enterprise (multiple email vendors, transactional providers, marketing tools). <2 includes = probably SMB.

**Cross-signal: distinct subdomains in CT logs.** Brands with >100 distinct subdomains in `crt.sh` = enterprise. Already partly tracked via CertStream.

**Schema additions:**
```sql
ALTER TABLE brands ADD COLUMN mx_provider TEXT;          -- 'google_workspace' | 'microsoft_365' | 'proofpoint' | etc.
ALTER TABLE brands ADD COLUMN size_estimate TEXT;         -- 'smb' | 'lower_mid' | 'mid' | 'upper_mid' | 'enterprise'
ALTER TABLE brands ADD COLUMN size_confidence REAL;       -- 0.0 - 1.0
ALTER TABLE brands ADD COLUMN size_signals_json TEXT;     -- raw signals: {mx_provider, spf_includes, subdomain_count, ...}
```

**Engineering effort:** ~1 week. Extends existing Cartographer phase 3 + email-security.ts. Run on existing brands as a backfill cron.

#### 8.7.2 Wikidata SPARQL ÔÇö strong for notable mid-market+

**Endpoint:** `https://query.wikidata.org/sparql`. Free, no auth, ~60 queries/minute. JSON response.

**Coverage:** ~50K-100K notable companies have website + employees + sector. Strong for known mid-market+; weak for true SMB.

**Sample query** ÔÇö given a domain like `docusign.com`, return company facts:

```sparql
SELECT ?company ?companyLabel ?employees ?revenue ?marketCap ?industry ?industryLabel ?inception ?countryLabel
WHERE {
  ?company wdt:P856 ?website .
  FILTER(REGEX(STR(?website), "(^|//|\\.)docusign\\.com($|/)", "i"))
  OPTIONAL { ?company wdt:P1128 ?employees . }     # employee count
  OPTIONAL { ?company wdt:P2139 ?revenue . }       # total revenue
  OPTIONAL { ?company wdt:P2226 ?marketCap . }     # market cap
  OPTIONAL { ?company wdt:P452 ?industry . }       # industry
  OPTIONAL { ?company wdt:P571 ?inception . }      # founding date
  OPTIONAL { ?company wdt:P17 ?country . }         # country
  SERVICE wikibase:label { bd:serviceParam wikibase:language "en". }
}
LIMIT 1
```

**Schema mapping:**
- `wdt:P1128` ÔåÆ `employee_count_est`
- `wdt:P2139` ÔåÆ `revenue_usd_est`
- `wdt:P2226` ÔåÆ `market_cap_usd_est`
- `wdt:P452` (industry label) ÔåÆ `industry_subsector`
- `wdt:P571` ÔåÆ `founded_year`

**Engineering effort:** ~3 days. Single fetch helper + JSON parser + schema upserts.

**Cache:** 90-day TTL; Wikidata data doesn't change fast.

#### 8.7.3 SEC EDGAR ÔÇö US public companies only

**Coverage:** ~10K US public companies. Narrow but high-quality data.

**Step 1 ÔÇö Bulk join:** download `https://www.sec.gov/files/company_tickers.json` weekly. Schema: `{cik, ticker, title}`. Match to our brands by name + ticker.

**Step 2 ÔÇö Per-brand XBRL fetch** (only for matched brands):

```
GET https://data.sec.gov/api/xbrl/companyconcept/CIK{cik:010d}/us-gaap/Revenues.json
GET https://data.sec.gov/api/xbrl/companyconcept/CIK{cik:010d}/dei/EntityCommonStockSharesOutstanding.json
GET https://data.sec.gov/submissions/CIK{cik:010d}.json   # most recent filings + employee count from 10-K
```

**Rate limits:** SEC requires `User-Agent` header and limits to 10 requests/second. Easy to stay within.

**Schema mapping:**
- `us-gaap/Revenues` (latest 10-K value) ÔåÆ `revenue_usd_est`
- `dei/EntityCommonStockSharesOutstanding` ├ù current price = `market_cap_usd_est`
- Employee count from 10-K narrative (regex "approximately N employees") ÔåÆ `employee_count_est`

**Engineering effort:** ~1 week. SEC ticker join cron + per-brand XBRL fetch + 10-K parser. Most fragile part is the employee count regex (varies by filer style).

**Use case:** primary source for US-listed mid-market+ ($100M+ revenue). Authoritative for revenue and market cap. Falls through to Wikidata for non-public.

#### 8.7.4 OpenCorporates ÔÇö entity verification + jurisdiction

**Endpoint:** `https://api.opencorporates.com/v0.4/companies/search?q={name}&country_code={cc}`. Free with API key (1,000 reads/month). Higher tiers paid.

**Coverage:** every registered legal entity globally. Jurisdiction-rich, but **does NOT include employee count, revenue, or market cap.**

**Use case:** entity verification + jurisdiction signal (GDPR scoping, regulated-industry classification). Useful for the legal-data side of customer onboarding, not size tiering.

**Schema mapping:**
- `jurisdiction_code` ÔåÆ `legal_jurisdiction`
- `incorporation_date` ÔåÆ `legal_inception_date`
- `registered_address.country` ÔåÆ `legal_country`
- `inactive` flag ÔåÆ filter out dead brands

**Engineering effort:** ~3 days. Search by name + cache by jurisdiction. **Defer to Phase 2** ÔÇö useful but not critical for size tiering.

#### 8.7.5 GitHub org API ÔÇö tech-sector employee proxy

**Endpoint:** `GET /orgs/{org}` (REST v3). Free, 60/hour unauthenticated, 5000/hour with PAT.

**Coverage:** tech-sector brands with public GitHub presence. Useless for non-tech.

**Signal:** `public_members` count is a rough employee proxy (typically 10-30% of actual employees show as public org members). `public_repos` count signals engineering scale.

**Schema mapping:**
- `public_members` ├ù ~5x ÔåÆ `employee_count_est` (low-confidence, only when no other source)
- Used as a *fallback signal* when Wikidata + SEC are null

**Engineering effort:** ~2 days. Useful primarily as a cross-check.

### 9.5 Action-button taxonomy ÔÇö first-class row actions

**Current state (verified):** `DataRow.tsx` is a bare primitive ÔÇö severity left-border + hover stripe + optional onClick. No built-in primary action, no kebab. Actions live either in detail panels (`Alerts.tsx` right column at `:522-545`) or above-row toolbars (`BrandDetail.tsx`). Inconsistent across the app.

**v3 convention:** every row has ONE primary action visible by default + a kebab menu for secondary actions. The primary action is the next step in the canonical workflow for that entity in its current state.

| Entity | Primary action | Kebab (secondary) actions |
|---|---|---|
| Threat | Request takedown | Mark FP ┬À Add to watchlist ┬À Pivot-link to actor ┬À Export STIX |
| Alert | Acknowledge | Dismiss ┬À Resolve ┬À Convert to incident ┬À Mute brand+type |
| Takedown | State-dependent: Approve / Submit / Escalate | View evidence ┬À Re-generate report ┬À Mark abandoned |
| Threat actor | Add to watchlist | Subscribe to updates ┬À Export profile ┬À Share with sector |
| Brand | Run scan | View health report ┬À Export brand brief ┬À Mute alert types |
| Provider | Open complaint | View scorecard ┬À Export to TIP ┬À Subscribe to changes |
| Lookalike | Request takedown | Add to safe-list ┬À Verify ownership ┬À Mark monitor-only |
| Abuse-mailbox item | Triage | Forward to T&S ┬À Close as benign ┬À Convert to alert |

**State-dependent primaries:** the takedown row computes its primary from current state ÔÇö `draft ÔåÆ Approve`, `approved ÔåÆ Submit`, `submitted+stalled>72h ÔåÆ Escalate`. Never renders a stale CTA.

**Implementation:** extend `DataRow.tsx` (`packages/averrow-ui/src/components/ui/DataRow.tsx`) to accept `primaryAction?: ButtonProps` and `kebabActions?: KebabAction[]`. Migrate existing call sites incrementally per restructure session ÔÇö `Threats.tsx`, `Alerts.tsx`, `BrandDetail.tsx` first.

**Wired today** (per Explore audit): acknowledge / dismiss / resolve alert (`useUpdateAlert`), bulk-acknowledge + bulk-takedown by brand_id, approve/reject lead, add safe domain, classify social profile, scan dark web.

**Gap ÔÇö five missing actions** (┬º3 gap inventory should reference this):
1. Per-row "Request takedown" (today: bulk only, only from alerts)
2. "Escalate" (today: not wired anywhere)
3. "Add to watchlist" (today: not wired anywhere)
4. "Export STIX per-row" (today: bulk export only, via `handlers/stixExport.ts`)
5. "Abuse-mailbox triage" (today: ┬º7 not built yet)

### 9.7 Persona split ÔÇö one app, two home layouts

┬º9.2.3 question: one platform for both T&S and threat-intel teams, or separate?

| Persona | Cadence | Mode | Home should lead with |
|---|---|---|---|
| T&S analyst (mid-market brand-protection) | Hourly ÔåÆ daily | Action-oriented | Zone 1 (Today) |
| Threat-intel lead (sector / geopolitical) | Daily ÔåÆ weekly | Narrative-oriented | Zone 2 (Narrative) |
| MSSP-style analyst (many small brands) | Continuous | Triage-oriented | Zone 1 + cross-tenant filter |

**Recommendation: one app, three home-screen layouts.** User-toggled persona at first sign-in. Default = T&S (highest-volume Pathfinder audience per ┬º8). Stored as `users.home_persona` enum. Same data, same nav, same actions ÔÇö only home zones reorder.

Implementation cost: one schema column + three home layout components sharing the same widgets. Not three apps.

**Open question for interviews:** does the persona split exist *between teams* or *within an analyst's day* (T&S in the morning, intel review in the afternoon)? If the latter, persona toggle becomes a top-bar control rather than a one-time setting.
